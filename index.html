What is James Webb Space Telescope?

The JWST is a large, space-based observatory optimized for infrared wavelengths, allowing it to peer through clouds of gas and dust and see further back in time than ever before. The telescope is designed to provide unprecedented images and data on the universe's earliest moments, star formation, and galaxy evolution.

Size and Design:
It features a large primary mirror that is 6.5 meters (about 21 feet) in diameter, much larger than Hubble’s 2.4-meter mirror. This large mirror allows it to collect more light and see fainter objects. The mirror is made up of 18 hexagonal segments that work together to focus light.

JWST: How Was it Made?

Development Timeline:

The project started in the late 1990s, with the aim to create a successor to the Hubble Space Telescope. The development took over two decades and involved numerous challenges, including technical hurdles and budget issues.

Launch:

JWST was launched on December 25, 2021, aboard an Ariane 5 rocket from French Guiana. After launch, it traveled about 1.5 million kilometers (about 930,000 miles) from Earth to its operating location at the second Lagrange point (L2), where it can maintain a stable position.
